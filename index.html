Our Mission

The fast fashion industry is one of the most polluting industries in the world. Many people are actively trying to buy clothes that are better for the planet but are deceived by big brands pretending to be more environmentally friendly than they are.

Our campaign aims to uncover deceptive practices in the fashion industry, promoting awareness about transparency in brands. At BEYOND THE LABEL we envision a future where young fashion enthusiasts are empowered with knowledge to make informed and sustainable choices.

Greenwash Guide

What is greenwashing?

Greenwashing, noun

The act or practice of making a product, policy, activity, etc. appear to be more environmentally friendly than it really is.

How to look ​“BEYOND THE LABEL” in 3 steps

A quick guide to shopping more consciously

  • Inform yourself: Familiarize yourself with greenwashing tactics and what defines sustainable practices. Some of the most common greenwashing tactics are:
    • Limited product Range: A brand may market a small line of products as sustainable while the majority of their offerings are not.
    • False certifications: Some companies may display certifications that are either irrelevant, outdated, or unverified. Check the credibility of certifications and verify their authenticity from reputable sources.
    • Vague and generic terms: Some brands use vague terms like "eco-friendly," "green," or "sustainable" without providing specific details or certifications.
    • Lack of transparency: Greenwashing often involves a lack of transparency regarding the production process and materials used. Genuine sustainable brands will provide detailed information about their supply chain, sourcing, and manufacturing practices. Check out the resource tap on our website to get more information on trustworthy sources about the fashion industry.
  • Assess the product Lifecycle: Evaluate a product's entire lifecycle, considering aspects from raw material extraction to disposal. Also take the environmental impact of materials and packaging into account.
  • Reflect on necessity: Before making a purchase, question whether you genuinely need the item. Acknowledge that the most sustainable piece of clothing might already be in your closet. To be sustainable, we need to fight overconsumption.
